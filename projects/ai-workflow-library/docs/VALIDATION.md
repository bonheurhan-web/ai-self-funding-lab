# AI Workflow Library Validation Pack

## Purpose

This pack defines the evidence required to decide whether `ai-workflow-library` may move from `validating` to `building`.

It is a research and decision document, not build authorization. No product code, public launch, payment integration, or production deployment is permitted by this document.

## 1. Target customer segments

1. Independent developers using AI to plan, implement, test, and operate software
2. Freelancers who want reusable automation for delivery and administration
3. AI tool users who need practical workflows rather than isolated prompts
4. Cursor, Codex, and ChatGPT users who want repeatable project systems
5. Solo founders who need lightweight operating, measurement, and monetization templates

## 2. Problem hypotheses

| ID | Hypothesized problem |
| --- | --- |
| P1 | Users do not know how to operate Codex as a persistent engineering employee instead of a one-shot code generator. |
| P2 | Users do not know how to structure an AI automation project so responsibilities, files, and approvals remain clear. |
| P3 | Users do not know how to make AI projects measurable with status, traffic, cost, revenue, profit, and ROI. |
| P4 | Users do not know how to write a useful `PROJECT.md` with scope, timebox, success metrics, and kill criteria. |
| P5 | Users do not know how to design an AI monetization experiment that tests revenue before building payment infrastructure. |
| P6 | Users struggle to make AI produce reusable assets and operating systems instead of scattered answers. |
| P7 | Users do not know how to write Cursor rules that are specific, maintainable, and safe for real projects. |
| P8 | Users find MCP server setup fragmented and lack a concise security and verification checklist. |
| P9 | Solo founders do not know which recurring tasks should be delegated to AI and which require manual approval. |
| P10 | Users lack a consistent daily workflow for assigning, reviewing, and measuring AI-agent work. |
| P11 | Users do not know when to pause or kill an AI project instead of continuing because of sunk cost. |
| P12 | Freelancers lack reusable AI delivery workflows that protect client data and prevent uncontrolled external actions. |

These are hypotheses. They are not evidence until supported by observable search, community, repository, competitor, or user behavior.

## 6. Validation methods

### GitHub README landing page

Prepare a repository README concept describing the problem, proposed assets, and calls to action. Do not publish a new repository or public landing page without approval.

Measure later:

- Qualified visits
- Asset link clicks
- Markdown downloads
- Stars, saves, or forks as secondary signals

### Simple waitlist form placeholder

Define a non-functional email-interest placeholder and its consent copy. Do not connect an email platform or collect addresses before Chairman approval.

Potential validation signal:

- Visitor explicitly requests launch notification or a specific asset

### PayPal support link placeholder

Reserve a clearly labeled support area in the proposed information architecture. Do not add a real PayPal URL or account identifier without Chairman approval.

Potential validation signal after approval:

- Support-link clicks
- Verified PayPal revenue greater than `$0`

### Gumroad, Payhip, or Ko-fi placeholder

Evaluate availability, mainland China operating compatibility, payout support, fees, digital-goods policies, and account requirements. These are placeholders only and are not approved monetization systems.

Do not create an account, publish a product, or accept platform terms without Chairman approval.

### Manual outreach message draft

Draft for later human review:

> I am validating a small library of reusable AI workflows for developers and solo founders. Which would save you more time: a Codex operating system, a measurable AI project dashboard, a Cursor rules pack, or an AI monetization checklist? I am looking for the specific task you currently rebuild or search for repeatedly.

Codex may draft variants but must not send them automatically.

### Community post drafts

Prepare separate drafts for Reddit, Hacker News, and Indie Hackers that:

- Lead with the problem and a useful artifact
- Ask for specific criticism rather than generic promotion
- Disclose project affiliation
- Follow each community's rules
- Avoid fabricated results or urgency

Codex must not post, comment, vote, message users, or represent the Chairman automatically.

### SEO keyword list

Maintain the keyword candidates in this document for later research. Before build approval:

- Expand to at least 20 high-intent candidates
- Map every retained keyword to a customer problem and proposed asset
- Identify likely intent: learn, compare, download, or buy
- Reject broad terms without a plausible conversion path

## 7. Build gate

Build phase may be recommended only when all conditions are satisfied:

- At least 20 high-intent keyword candidates are documented and mapped to problems and assets
- At least five downloadable template assets have complete one-page definitions
- At least three publishable acquisition channels are identified with channel-specific validation plans
- At least one explicit first-revenue path is documented from acquisition to verified revenue
- Evidence contains at least three independent demand signals
- A ranked MVP contains no more than three launch assets
- Free and paid value boundaries are explicit
- Measurement events and the 30-day reporting window are defined
- Chairman confirms that a PayPal support link or approved payment link is available for the planned experiment
- Chairman approves any public publishing, account action, spending, or deployment
- ChatGPT explicitly approves entry into build phase
- Dashboard status remains `validating` until the approval decision is recorded

If any gate is missing, the decision is `no build`.

## 8. Kill and pause criteria

### Kill

- No one visits an approved landing page during a 14-day measurement period despite executing the approved distribution plan
- During a 30-day measurement period there are no downloads, email-interest signals, or support-link clicks
- Research cannot identify a clear reason to pay for any bundle
- Proposed assets have no meaningful differentiation from strong free alternatives
- Fewer than three independent demand signals are found
- Acquisition depends on prohibited spam, misleading claims, or unapproved paid distribution
- First revenue requires prohibited payment infrastructure

### Pause

- Analytics cannot distinguish real demand from internal or automated traffic
- PayPal or another approved verification source cannot be confirmed
- Community rules, licensing, privacy, payment, or platform-policy questions remain unresolved
- Required Chairman or ChatGPT review is unavailable by the review date
- Candidate assets need security or accuracy review before exposure

## 9. Manual approval requirements

Codex must not:

- Automatically publish Reddit, Hacker News, Indie Hackers, or other community posts
- Automatically send outreach messages, emails, comments, or direct messages
- Automatically connect any payment API
- Automatically add a real PayPal, affiliate, Gumroad, Payhip, Ko-fi, or AdSense account
- Automatically buy a domain, subscription, API, advertisement, or other service
- Automatically deploy a production environment
- Automatically collect email addresses or personal information
- Automatically change project status to `building`

These actions require explicit Chairman approval. Entry into build phase additionally requires explicit ChatGPT approval.
