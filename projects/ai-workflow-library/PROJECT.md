## 1. Customer and problem

### Target customers

- Independent developers using ChatGPT, Claude, Codex, or Cursor
- Freelancers who want to automate repetitive work
- People learning practical AI workflows
- Builders who want to copy and adapt executable AI operating systems

### Problem

Useful AI workflows are scattered across posts, chats, repositories, and videos. They are often incomplete, difficult to evaluate, or not packaged for immediate reuse. Users spend time reconstructing prompts, rules, checklists, and operating procedures instead of applying them.

### Current alternatives

- Searching social media, blogs, GitHub, and video tutorials
- Writing prompts and operating documents from scratch
- Buying broad prompt bundles with unclear quality or maintenance
- Copying undocumented workflows from other projects

## 2. Proposed solution

A static, searchable library of focused AI workflows and templates that can be previewed on the web and downloaded as Markdown.

Initial candidate assets:

- Codex project operating system template
- AI startup dashboard template
- AI workflow prompt pack
- Cursor rules template
- MCP server setup checklist
- AI monetization experiment checklist
- Solo founder automation checklist

Each released asset should state its intended user, prerequisites, expected output, usage steps, and limitations.

### Technical recommendation

Use a static-first site. Next.js is the preferred initial choice because the company already has tested operational knowledge for static export, but Astro remains acceptable if validation shows a meaningful simplicity advantage.

No framework or product code may be created before build approval.

## 3. Acquisition hypothesis

Free, useful assets can acquire qualified users through:

- Search pages targeting specific workflow and template intent
- GitHub discovery and links to downloadable Markdown
- Relevant developer and solo-founder communities where self-promotion is permitted
- Organic sharing of individual templates
- Cross-links from future AI Self-Funding Lab reports and experiments

The hypothesis is that narrowly named, immediately reusable assets will outperform generic AI advice in search intent, saves, downloads, and referrals.

## 4. Monetization hypothesis

Phase 1 follows `docs/MONETIZATION.md`.

Priority order:

1. Free web library and free Markdown downloads to establish demand
2. Optional static PayPal support link after Chairman approval

## 9. Kill criteria

### Stop

- Validation does not identify three independent demand signals within 7 days
- No candidate asset has a credible acquisition channel
- Monetization would require a prohibited payment system
- The project cannot remain static and operationally simple
- After an approved 30-day measurement window, there is no qualified traffic, download activity, monetization interaction, or actionable learning

### Pause

- Required account access or approval is unavailable
- Measurement cannot reliably attribute traffic or revenue
- A legal, licensing, platform-policy, or payment-compliance question remains unresolved

### Scale

- Level 1 revenue is verified and repeatable demand signals exist
- One or more assets show sustained qualified acquisition
- The paid bundle or affiliate path has evidence without requiring prohibited infrastructure
