# Proposed: Approve AI Workflow Library Minimum Presale Page

## Status

`proposed`

This record is a draft. It does not approve build, publishing, deployment, payment collection, outreach, or spending.

## Context

The `ai-workflow-library` project has documented public problem evidence and now has a proposed `$5` presale package. A complete product site remains unjustified. A minimal static page may be a lower-cost way to test whether target users understand the offer and may pay for the connected template system.

## Why consider approving a minimum page

- Five proposed assets now have defined free and paid boundaries.
- The `$5` package specifies concrete downloadable files.
- Public evidence supports workflow, handoff, rules, review, and project-structure problems.
- A single static page can test messaging without a database, login, subscription, or payment API.
- The page can preserve the company's static-first and zero-required-spend constraints.

## What is still missing

- Chairman confirmation that a PayPal support or payment link is operational and appropriate for the experiment
- ChatGPT approval of the exact minimum build scope
- Chairman approval for public publishing and production deployment
- Evidence from at least five target-user reviews or equivalent behavioral validation
- Final measurement events and reporting window
- Final delivery timing, refund handling, and license language for any actual presale
- Proof that the paid boundary is meaningfully better than strong free competitors

## Decision

`proposed`

Do not move the dashboard status from `validating` to `building` unless all approval conditions are recorded.

## If approved, Codex may

- Create one minimal static page from the approved copy
- Include free-download descriptions and non-sensitive placeholder files
- Include the `$5` package description
- Include disabled PayPal and affiliate placeholders
- Add privacy-safe measurement specified in the approval
- Add tests, metadata, accessibility, and CI required for the single page
- Prepare deployment configuration without deploying until separate Chairman approval

## Codex may not

- Add or expose a real PayPal, affiliate, AdSense, or email account without Chairman approval
- Build a payment API, checkout, subscription, database, login, CMS, or administration system
- Use Stripe, cryptocurrency, or a merchant-of-record integration
- Publish community posts, send outreach, or contact users automatically
- Buy a domain, service, advertisement, API, or subscription
- Deploy to production without explicit Chairman approval
- Claim verified demand, buyers, or revenue before evidence exists
- Expand beyond the approved single-page scope

## Chairman must confirm

- Whether the PayPal support or digital-download payment link is available
- Which link, if any, may be displayed and how account information will be protected
- Whether a presale is allowed before all files are complete
- Delivery timing, refund handling, and customer-support process
- Whether the page may be publicly published
- Whether production deployment is approved
- Whether any target-user outreach or community post may be sent
- Maximum permitted spend, which remains `$0` unless explicitly changed

## Expected result

A tightly scoped page should produce clearer evidence about message comprehension, asset interest, `$5` willingness to pay, and the viability of static PayPal-based monetization before any broader product build.

## Cost and risk

- Expected mandatory external cost: `$0`
- Engineering risk: scope expansion into a content site or checkout system
- Commercial risk: users may value free files but reject the paid bundle
- Compliance risk: unclear presale, refund, licensing, or PayPal rules
- Mitigation: one-page scope, explicit placeholders, manual approvals, truthful status, and no payment API
